Introduction

Esports in League of Legends

Thanks to the attractiveness and unique mechanics in League of Legends it has quickly become the most popular esports game in the world and brings together millions of players around the globe. Riot Games constantly supports professional games at every level - from the international LCS, LEC or LPL leagues, to more local ones, like the Polish Ultraliga. The studio also collaborates with the most popular Streamers and Youtubers from around the globe, granting them additional perks and skins to give away, and even closer to the headquarters, offering testing of new and experimental content long before the premiere for official servers.

There is a number of different services online that allow you to follow esports events in League of Legends such as YouTube and Twitch as well as some proprietary services however these are beyond the scope of this guide. It is important to emphasize that there are different esports leagues based on the geographic location for example American, Asian or European. All of the esports leagues follow their own streaming schedule which means that some esports events are transmitted only during certain days of the week in particular regions. The current esports schedule can be checked on an official page of an esports league or on lol.fancdom.com (more information about this can be found under the "Useful Websites" section).

Introduction

Highest Tier Leagues

The esports hierarchy in League of Legends consists of two separate levels (primary and secondary). The primary tier which is the highest esports distinction in League of Legends is reserved only for the best players from a given region. There are currently 13 primary tier leagues in League of Legends (see the attached map for more details). For example, LEC is the European primary tier region that affiliates the best players from the European continent. These kinds of players compete with each other in official league matches during an esports season (normally there is the spring esports season between January and March and the summer esports season between June and August).

Upon each season's end 3 best teams from a primary league who manage to score the highest number of wins against other teams from the respected primary tier league qualify for international tournaments. The Mid-Season Invitational (MSI) tournament takes place after the spring season and it lasts till May. During MSI the best teams from each region compete against each other in order to win the MSI trophy and recognition on the international stage. The second tournament is called the World Championship and it takes place after the summer season. The World Championship is considered the biggest and most prestigious tournament in League of Legends where the best teams from each region fight for the title of the best team in the world and attractive prize money of several million dollars.

Introduction

Secondary Tier Leagues

The secondary tier leagues are usually reserved for players who were not able to secure an esports contract with a team from the primary tier but are still considered to be one of the best players in their respective countries. These kinds of players normally compete with each other in country-specific leagues for example in Poland there is a secondry tier league called "Ultraliga" that is a home for the best players from Poland. Similarly, players from primary tier leagues, the players compete with each other in official league matches during an esports season (normally there is the spring esports season between January and March and the summer esports season between June and August).

Upon each season's end 3 best teams from a secondary league that manage to score the highest number of wins against other teams from the respected secondary tier league qualify for an international tournament consisting of the best second-tier teams from a given region. For instance, in Europe such a tournament is called "European Masters" and it takes place twice a year at the end of an esports season. The qualified teams compete with one another to win the title of the European Masters champion and attractive prize money of several thousand euros. Apart from that, the best players from each team have a chance to show off their skills on the international stage to scouts from primary league teams.
