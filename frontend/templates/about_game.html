Introduction

Game Objectives

League of Legends is a MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) strategy game. Two teams of 5 or 3 different heroes face each other, collecting gold and experience to use their advantage to win. The goal of the game is to destroy the most important point in the enemy base - the Nexus. This can be achieved by breaking through enemy fortifications or by forcing them to surrender.

In every League of Legends match, the team must set itself the task of scoring new goals. Although the game can last almost an hour, the end result will always be a destroyed Nexus on one side of the barricade. However, in order to reach the main enemy base, you have to wade through smaller, but still important fortifications.

Introduction

Available Roles and Heroes

Regardless of the chosen hero, the player must choose 1 of 5 available roles to play during the match. These are dictated by the lines drawn on the game map, and there are Top Lane, Jungle, Middle Lane, Bottom Lane, and Support each role completely different from the rest. In order to climb the ranks of the ranking, it is worth focusing on two roles and learning the rest in a basic way.

In addition to the number of distinct roles available for players in League of Legends they can also choose one of over 140 unique heroes. While each of them may have a unique set of skills, they can be assigned to specific Classes. They are divided into Defenders, Warriors, Conquerors, Mages, Creators and Riflemen. Each of these classes represents a given style of play and a pattern of player behavior. This division makes it easier for Summoners to find their way around the situation and allows them to easily choose their favorite role in the game.

Introduction

Game Ranking

The ranking system in League of Legends consists of 9 leagues, and these are divided into 4 divisions (IV - the weakest, I - the strongest). The league type and divisions represent groups of players with similar skill levels. Players win ranking points for winning matches, and for losing them. If a player in a given division scores 100 points, he will be admitted to matches that may promote him to a higher division. To get a promotion between divisions you will have to win 2 out of 3 consecutive skirmishes. If a player is struggling to get promoted to a higher league, he will have to win 3 out of 5 competitions. To make things easier, players who lose their promotion to a higher division will start with one win at the next. This rule works only below the Golden I league.

The League of Master, Grandmaster and Challenger has its own rules. There are no divisions there, only ranking points. The more points, the higher the position in the table. Players do not advance through the promotion system - each division has a maximum summoner pool, and getting into it requires reaching a certain point threshold. This kind of ranking includes only the best of the best players who are already professional esports players or who aspire to be selected to a professional esports team.
